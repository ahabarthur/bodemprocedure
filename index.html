Family court — preparation notes

50-50 Custody — Argument Map

Establishing that an irregular pilot roster does not undermine stability for the children (ages 4 and 6). Working framework — confirm procedural specifics with your advocaat.

1
An irregular roster means no stable routine.

My evidence — I can deal with it

  • Airline rosters are published weeks in advance — document how far ahead yours publishes.
  • A written, concrete care plan mapping duty days to who has the children each day.
  • The children's environment stays constant (own home, own beds, same school).

Counter-argument

"Stability means the same parent present daily; he can't provide that."

Evidence to deconstruct it

  • Define stability correctly: predictability, emotional security, consistent environment — not an identical daily schedule.
  • A sample roster-to-care calendar (one representative month) showing the plan works in practice.
  • Many "normal" jobs (on-call, overtime, last-minute travel) are less predictable than a published roster.
2
When he flies, his mother raises the children, not him.

My evidence — I can deal with it

  • Grandmother lives 15 min away, reliable on short notice, already known and loved by the children.
  • Position her as a named safety net and supplement, documented in the care plan.
  • A short written statement from your mother confirming her role and availability.

Counter-argument

"This is grandmother-care dressed up as father-care — he isn't really asking for 50-50."

Evidence to deconstruct it

  • Show the majority of custodial time is genuinely you (depends on roster type — see Issue 3).
  • Records of direct, hands-on caregiving: meals, bath, bedtime, school runs, medical appointments.
  • Frame grandmother as family continuity (kids stay in their kin network), not a paid substitute.
3
Multi-day trips mean repeated overnights away from the children.

My evidence — I can deal with it

  • Continuity of caregiver and environment: children stay in their home with a consistent, trusted adult.
  • Good-faith roster planning — bidding for clustered trips, requesting roster stability for family reasons.

Counter-argument

"Two very young children need a parent present overnight; grandma is carrying core parenting."

Evidence to deconstruct it

  • If short-haul: show duties are largely same-day / single-overnight; align "on" weeks with lighter roster periods.
  • If long-haul: document requests for roster accommodation; propose a substantial-but-realistic split if honesty requires it.
  • Show overnights away are limited in number and predictable, not random or frequent.
4
Young children (4 and 6) especially need routine.

My evidence — I can deal with it

  • Both are in basisschool — school is an external stability anchor regardless of which parent has them.
  • A large part of any weekday is school, not parenting, for either parent.

Counter-argument

"Precisely because they're young, disruption is more harmful."

Evidence to deconstruct it

  • Your trips largely overlap with school hours — minimal disruption to the children's actual day.
  • Constant anchors regardless of your roster: same home, school, teachers, grandmother, friends.
  • Evidence of the children's secure bond with you (settled room, belongings, daily involvement).

Core message to the court

The children's environment stays constant — their home, school, grandmother, and a father present the large majority of the time — and the published-in-advance nature of the work means continuity can be planned more reliably than for a parent with unpredictable hours.
