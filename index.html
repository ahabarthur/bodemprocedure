Custody and Parenting — Argument Map

Establishing that a long-haul roster does not undermine stability for the children (ages 4 and 6). Pattern: trips of 3–6 days (averaging ~4.5), each followed by an equal stretch at home; the children are with me on my home days, which are known 4–5 weeks in advance. This gives close to equal nights and frequent, predictable father contact. Working framework — confirm procedural specifics with your advocaat. The proposal gives the children close to equal nights with each parent. My time with them falls on my home days — short trips of three to six days, then an equal stretch at home — all known four to five weeks in advance. Separations are brief and predictable, daily contact continues during trips, my home days are entirely off duty, and one home and school stay constant throughout. The result is frequent, concentrated father contact that most working parents cannot match.

Family court — conduct and safety

Conduct and Safety — Argument Map

Documenting conduct relevant to the children’s safety and wellbeing, and answering allegations, with factual, dated, verifiable records. The court weighs conduct only as it affects the children. Unsupported or exaggerated claims damage credibility; route genuine safety concerns through the proper authorities (Veilig Thuis, the huisarts, or police). Working framework — confirm procedural specifics with your advocaat. Keep every entry factual, dated, and tied to a concrete effect on the children; rely on neutral, independent corroboration over personal characterisation; and let documented patterns — not heated language — make the point. On contested conduct, restraint and verifiability are what persuade.
